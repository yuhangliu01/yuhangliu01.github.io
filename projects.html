Current and Ongoing Research Initiatives

Active Research Projects

Correlation to Causality (C2C) Route

Long-term Research Agenda

A systematic framework exploring how learning systems progressively transition from identifying correlations to establishing causal relationships through data diversity. This project encompasses multiple sub-themes:

  • Data Diversity: Understanding how diverse data representations facilitate the discovery of causal structures
  • Causal Discovery: Developing methods to identify true causal relationships from observational data
  • Causal Reasoning: Building systems that can perform robust inference under causal assumptions
  • Theoretical Foundations: Establishing theoretical guarantees for causal inference in diverse data regimes

LLM Interpretability and Reasoning

Current Focus

Investigating the mechanisms behind interpretability, controllability, reasoning, and planning in large language models. Key research directions include:

  • Concept Learning: Understanding what concepts LLMs learn from next token prediction
  • Interpretability: Developing methods to explain LLM decision-making processes
  • Controllability: Creating techniques to guide and constrain LLM behavior
  • Reasoning Mechanisms: Analyzing how LLMs perform complex reasoning tasks

Single-Cell Perturbation Prediction

Collaborative Research

Developing advanced machine learning methods for predicting cellular responses to genetic perturbations. This project bridges computational biology and deep learning:

  • Representation Learning: Identifying optimal representations for cell state modeling
  • Causal Modeling: Understanding causal relationships in gene regulatory networks
  • Prediction Accuracy: Improving accuracy of cellular response predictions to interventions
  • Biological Insights: Extracting biological knowledge from learned models

Latent Causal Models

Theoretical Research

Advancing the theory and practice of learning causal models in latent spaces. Focus areas include:

  • Weight-Variant Models: Identifying causal structures with variable edge weights
  • Identifiability: Establishing conditions under which latent causal models are identifiable
  • Efficient Learning: Developing sample-efficient algorithms for causal discovery
  • Robustness: Ensuring learned models generalize across different data distributions
